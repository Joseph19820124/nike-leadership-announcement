Angela Dong's Farewell Letter | Angela Dong 告别信

From: Angela Dong | VP/GM, Greater China | 大中华区副总裁/总经理

English

To our incredible team, partners, and stakeholders,

After more than 20 of the most rewarding and transformative years of my life, I have decided the time is right for me to transition from Nike and pass the baton leading Nike's Greater China geography.

It has been the deepest privilege to lead this company alongside each of you. Together, we've built more than a business; we've built a mission-driven community and a culture of resilience and innovation I will always cherish.

As I reflect on our time together, I feel immense gratitude for what this team has accomplished. We helped grow sport in ways that genuinely touched people's lives, from empowering women to supporting young athletes through the Shanghai Marathon, ADT, Chongli 168, and CHBL/HBL. We've been championing and serving Chinese athletes like Liu Xiang, Li Na, Yi Jianlian, Su Bingtian, Zheng Qinwen and many others through every triumph and challenge.

We've built a connected, consumer centered retail ecosystem across 5,000 stores and 120,000 athletes. And we supported local communities in need through programs such as Boundless Girls and Jordan Wings - creating moments of opportunity, confidence, and joy. These memories and the impact we made together are ones I will carry with me always.

To ensure the smoothest possible handover, my final weeks will be dedicated to supporting this transition. As I pass the baton and departure from day-to-day operations, my belief in this company and my pride in what we've built together will remain unwavering. I will be cheering for your continued success from the sidelines as an alumna and Nike's biggest supporter.

I want to express my complete and unwavering confidence in Cathy Sparks. I know she is deeply committed to our purpose and is the right leader to guide you to your next great achievements.

Please extend your full energy and the same dedication you have shown me to Cathy. The future here is extraordinarily bright.

I look forward to celebrating and saying farewell with you in the following weeks.

With immense gratitude and optimism,

Angela

Leadership Announcement | 领导层公告

From: Elliott Hill | President & CEO, NIKE, Inc. | 总裁兼首席执行官

Date: January 21, 2026

English

Team,

In recent months, the Senior Leadership Team has benefited from having our Geography VP/GMs at the table helping us move faster and bringing us closer to athletes* in both countries and marketplaces around the world.

As we continue to drive our Win Now actions and stand up our Sport Offense, I'd like to share several important leadership changes across three of our Geo VP/GM roles.

EMEA

Carl Grebert, a true legend on our team, has decided to retire after nearly 30 years of service and leadership across global, country and geography roles across the company. Carl shared his thinking with me some time ago, and I'm deeply grateful he helped guide us through my first year and thoughtfully worked with us to identify his successor.

If you look at what we've proven since—that athletes* don't just endorse culture, they shape it; that taking a point of view like Just Do It beats playing it safe; and that sport is a mirror of society, not just an escape from it—you'll find Carl in the stands, on the field, and in the highlight reels of this company.

César Garcia will become our new VP/GM of EMEA, effective February 2.

César started at Nike nearly 25 years ago as an EKIN in Spain and has since built a critical ability to connect product, sport, and marketplace into one integrated system.

GREATER CHINA

I also want to share that Angela Dong, who currently leads our Greater China geography, has decided to pass the baton and transition from her role and the company, effective March 31.

Angela joined Nike in 2005 and has held a range of increasingly senior roles across Greater China for more than 20 years. Her tenure and leadership have spanned pivotal moments for sport, culture, and our business in this geo, including the Beijing Olympics, the rapid rise of the Chinese consumer and digital ecosystem and the unprecedented disruption and recovery connected to the global pandemic.

Throughout her more than two decades with Nike, Angela has earned a reputation for strong leadership, deep knowledge of the marketplace and the business and helping steward us through complexity and change.

Cathy Sparks will step into the role of VP/GM, Greater China.

Cathy is a 25-year Nike veteran who began her journey as a store athlete at what was then Niketown Portland and went on to lead teams across every geography. Most recently, she served as VP/GM of APLA.

APLA

Cristin "Crissy" Campbell will serve as Interim VP/GM of APLA and join the Senior Leadership Team.

Crissy has spent more than 15 years at Nike – eight of those in APLA across a range of business leadership roles.

As we live our Maxim to Be on the Offense Always, I'm confident these changes will further accelerate our Sport Offense, advance our Win Now actions, and position NIKE, Inc. to continue having impact in the way only we can.

My best,

Elliott Hill
President & CEO, NIKE, Inc.

此致，

Global Merchandising Leadership | 全球商品领导层公告

From: Amy Montagne | President, Nike | Nike总裁

Date: January 2026

English

Team –

I wanted to take a moment to build on Elliott's message and congratulate César, Cathy, and Crissy on their new and important roles — and to thank Carl and Angela for all they've done on behalf of Nike. It's always our goal to see teammates step into new and exciting opportunities, but it's also bittersweet to say goodbye to such impactful leaders.

VP, Global Merchandising

With César moving into his new role as VP/GM, EMEA, I'm pleased to share that Erin Moscow will join the Nike Brand Leadership Team as VP, Global Merchandising, reporting to me and overseeing Merchandising as well as Business Integration and Sports Analytics.

In this role, Erin will set the direction for Nike's global merchandising strategy. She will work closely with product, global, and geo merchandising teams to shape the line plan and deliver consistent, marketplace right assortment frameworks that serve athletes* globally, while accelerating operational excellence and cross functional integration.

Erin Moscow — 20+ years of merchandising experience across the apparel and sport industry with a relentless focus on the consumer.

With more than 20 years of merchandising experience, Erin has built an impressive global career across the apparel and sport industry, always with a relentless focus on the consumer. Since joining Nike in 2014, she has held leadership roles across Global, North America, EMEA, and APLA — shaping product strategies that fuel growth, strengthen brand distinction, and meet the evolving needs of athletes and consumers worldwide.

Beyond her passion for product, storytelling, and brand connection, Erin is a people first leader who is committed to developing the next generation of merchandising talent. Originally from Canada, Erin, her husband Elliot, and their daughters, Madison and Maisie, have lived around the world and maintain a deep connection to sport, with family passions that span skiing, fencing, soccer, lacrosse, and more.

Erin will begin in her new role February 2 and a backfill for Direct & Brand Impact Sport Merchandising will be announced soon.

Change is never easy, but at Nike it's constant — it fuels our growth and inspires our future. As we look forward, please join me in congratulating Erin, César, Cathy, and Crissy on their new roles, and in thanking Carl and Angela for all they've done to serve athletes*, grow our marketplaces, and push the boundaries of sport like no other brand can.

Amy Montagne
President, Nike
